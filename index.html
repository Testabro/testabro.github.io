About

I was always interested in computing and networked systems even growing up in the woods of Vermont. When I was 18 I left home to serve as an electronics technician in the United States Navy. I am grateful for the opportunities this service provided me. This was my first formal education and application in electronic RF->digital communication. This also provided me opportunity to further my education in computer information systems that lead to my first job outside of the Navy configuring and deploying a national secure network platform.

My interest moved toward system development and software. The time was right, and I took up a position working as a systems and software integrator on a classified self-contained mission system. I solved techincal problems and contributed to the product using bash, python, and a bit of java. At this point I completed a milestone in my formal college education in software development and computer systems security. I was ready for my next opportunity to grow and contribute to a new project.

I was lucky enough to get an opportunity to put my interests and skills in software defined systems development and networks to work. Although this new job was not a direct development job it was a software defined satellite communication network platform that I had direct influence in identifying and fixing defects. In addition, it was very much defect and troubleshooting oriented with problems at the forefront of much of the day-to-day taskings. I used at my inner entrepreneur in order to develop and deploy scripts to work around problems and reduce toil.
